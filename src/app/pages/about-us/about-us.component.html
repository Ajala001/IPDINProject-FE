Background

IPDIN was created to address the lack of formal training for driver instructors in Nigeria. Poorly trained instructors contribute to road accidents. IPDIN is here to change that. It was founded in response to the lack of formal education for driver instructors in Nigeria. Road accidents are often the result of poorly trained instructors. IPDIN aims to change this by training professionals using certified curricula and modern teaching techniques.

Our Aim

Raise the safety awareness of road users by offering professional instruction and education.

Vision

To professionalize driving instruction through certified training that produces safer drivers.

Our Objectives

  • Training via seminars & workshops
  • Stay up-to-date with industry trends
  • Government & NGO collaboration
  • Promote global cooperation
  • Maintain a public instructor registry

Code of Professional Conduct

1

Uphold integrity, honesty, and transparency

2

Share knowledge through seminars and mentorship

3

Be responsible to learners, institutions, and the public

4

Deliver standardized, high-quality training

5

Prioritize safety in all instruction activities

6

Avoid unhealthy competition and professional conflicts

7

Reject corruption, malpractice, and favoritism

8

Promote continuous learning professional development

9

Respect diversity and avoid discrimination

Funding & Finance

IPDIN is supported through member dues, training fees, donations, and collaborations with stakeholders from public and private sectors.
